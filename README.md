# Mukto-Pakhi---Android-Application

Mukto Pakhi is an Android-based mobile application designed to combat child forced labor, exploitation, and abuse in Bangladesh. Developed as a youth-led civic tech solution, the app creates a secure digital bridge between vulnerable children, their communities, and the organizations responsible for protecting them.
Key Features

Anonymous & Secure Reporting: Allows children, witnesses, or concerned citizens to report cases of child labor, physical/sexual abuse, or unsafe working conditions without revealing their identity, reducing fear of retaliation.
Evidence Collection & Sharing: Users can attach photos, videos, geolocation data, and other proof to strengthen reports.
Real-time Case Tracking: Reporters can follow the progress of their submitted cases in a transparent manner.
Multi-Stakeholder Collaboration: The platform connects users directly with relevant NGOs, child rights organizations, volunteers, law enforcement authorities, and government bodies to enable faster response, coordinated rescue operations, and follow-up support (such as rehabilitation and legal aid).
User-Friendly Design: Built keeping in mind low-literacy users and limited internet connectivity common in vulnerable areas.

# Achievement in PTIB Civic Tech Challenge 2025
Mukto Pakhi participated in the PTIB Civic Tech Challenge 2025, a national innovation competition organized under the "Partnerships for a More Tolerant and Inclusive Bangladesh (PTIB)" project. This initiative was jointly implemented by:

▪️UNDP Bangladesh
▪️ICT Division, Ministry of Posts, Telecommunications and Information Technology
▪️Bangladesh Computer Council (BCC)
▪️With support from the Embassy of Norway

The challenge invited youth innovators and developers to create tech-driven solutions addressing governance, inclusion, human rights, and social issues. Out of 300+ participating projects, Mukto Pakhi was selected as one of the top 9 finalists.
The final round and Civic Tech Innovation Expo took place on 12 April 2026 at the BCC Auditorium in Dhaka. The event showcased Bangladesh’s emerging youth-led civic tech ecosystem and brought together government officials, development partners, and innovators.

For its strong social impact and technical merit, Mukto Pakhi received national recognition and was awarded BDT 150,000 Taka funding from UNDP Bangladesh. Some sources also highlight it securing a Top 3 position among the civic tech solutions presented.
